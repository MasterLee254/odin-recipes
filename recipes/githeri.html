Githeri

Description

Githeri is a traditional Kenyan dish that consists of boiled maize and beans, and it can be enjoyed as a main meal or a side dish. It is nutritious, hearty, and often spiced with vegetables and seasonings to enhance its flavor. Here's a basic recipe to make githeri.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried maize (corn kernels) or 2 cans of whole kernel corn (drained)
  • 2 cups dried beans (red kidney beans or any preferred type) or 2 cans of beans (drained)
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 inches ginger, minced
  • 3 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 carrots, diced
  • 1-2 green bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas (optional)
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or coriander for garnish

Preparation Steps

  1. For dried maize and beans: Soak them separately overnight. The next day, drain and rinse them.
  2. For canned maize and beans: Simply drain and rinse them.
  3. In a large pot, combine the soaked maize and beans.
  4. Add enough water to cover the maize and beans by about an inch.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the maize and beans are tender. This could take about 1-2 hours for dried maize and beans. If using canned, this step is quicker, about 20-30 minutes.
  6. In a separate large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  7. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown.
  8. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  9. Add the ground cumin, curry powder, and paprika to the pan. Stir well to combine and let the spices cook for about a minute.
  10. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and start to break down into a sauce.
  11. Add the diced carrots, green bell peppers, and peas (if using) to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  12. Add the cooked maize and beans to the pan, mixing well to combine with the vegetables and spices.
  13. Add salt to taste and a little water if needed to create a slightly saucy consistency.
  14. Let the mixture simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  15. Garnish with fresh cilantro or coriander.
  16. Serve hot, either on its own or with a side of chapati, rice, or any preferred accompaniment.

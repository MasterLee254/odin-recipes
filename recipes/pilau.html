Pilau

Description

Pilau is a popular rice dish in East Africa, particularly in Kenya and Tanzania. It's known for its rich, aromatic spices and is often served during special occasions and gatherings. Here's a basic recipe to make a delicious pot of pilau.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef, chicken, or goat, cut into chunks (optional for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 inches ginger, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5-6 cardamom pods
  • 5-6 cloves
  • 1-2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric (optional, for color)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 potatoes, peeled and cubed (optional)
  • 4 cups beef, chicken, or vegetable broth (or water)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps to remove excess starch and prevent the rice from becoming sticky.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat.
  3. Add the meat and cook until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  5. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Add the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground black pepper, and bay leaves.
  7. Stir well and let the spices toast for a minute to release their aromas.
  8. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and start to break down.
  9. If using potatoes, add them now and stir to coat with the spices.
  10. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to coat the grains with the spices.
  11. Return the browned meat to the pot (if using).
  12. Pour in the broth or water and add salt to taste. Stir well.
  13. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid.
  14. Let the pilau simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid. Avoid lifting the lid during cooking to ensure the rice steams properly.
  15. Once the rice is cooked, turn off the heat and let the pot sit, covered, for an additional 5-10 minutes.
  16. emove the lid and fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  17. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  18. Serve hot, with a side of kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad) or a simple vegetable dish.
